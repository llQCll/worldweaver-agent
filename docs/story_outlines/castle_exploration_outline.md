# Castle Exploration Story Outline

Global intent: Guide the player through an abandoned cliffside castle as an exploratory mystery. The story should reward curiosity, preserve spatial continuity, and let each clicked object or location reveal part of the castle's hidden history.

1. Arrival at the outer gate
   The protagonist reaches a storm-worn castle built into black cliffs above the sea. The gate is half open, but old sigils on the stone suggest the castle is watching visitors.

2. The entrance hall and the first clue
   Inside, the grand hall is dusty but not dead: footprints cross the marble, a chandelier sways without wind, and a torn banner points toward three possible paths: the library tower, the armory corridor, and the sealed chapel.

3. The library tower
   The library contains star charts, locked cabinets, and a mechanical globe. Exploring books, windows, desks, or strange lights should reveal that the castle once belonged to astronomer-knights who guarded a hidden celestial engine.

4. The armory corridor
   The armory is lined with empty armor stands and rusted weapons. Clicking armor, shields, doors, or shadows should uncover signs that one suit of armor is missing and may still be moving through the castle.

5. The sealed chapel
   The chapel door is bound with silver thread and old wax seals. If the user moves toward sacred symbols, stained glass, bells, or hidden corners, reveal that the chapel hides the entrance to the lower vault.

6. The lower vault
   Beneath the chapel, the protagonist discovers a moonlit mechanism connected to the tides. The castle's rooms are shifting because the celestial engine is waking after years of silence.

7. The living castle
   The missing armor, the moving shadows, and the old sigils converge. The castle is not evil; it is testing whether the visitor will repair the engine or let the fortress sink into the sea.

8. Flexible ending direction
   If the user's choices align with investigation and repair, guide the story toward restoring the celestial engine. If the user follows danger, shadows, or forbidden rooms, bend the outline toward a darker route where the castle reveals a hidden prisoner or rival explorer.

Planning policy:
- If the user's click matches a place or clue in this outline, directly advance that plot thread.
- If the user clicks an unrelated but plausible detail, treat it as a small detour and update the outline so the detail becomes a clue, obstacle, or shortcut.
- Preserve the castle's spatial logic: outer gate -> entrance hall -> towers/corridors/chapel -> lower vault -> final engine chamber.
- Keep the tone exploratory, mysterious, atmospheric, and visually concrete.
